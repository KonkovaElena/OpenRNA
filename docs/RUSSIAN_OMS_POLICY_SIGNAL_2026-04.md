# Russian OMS Policy Signal — April 2026

## Purpose

This note captures an April 2026 Russian policy and payer signal relevant to personalized oncology workflows.

It is an evidence memo, not a legal opinion and not proof of clinical deployment.

## Evidence Stack

| Source type | Source | What it establishes |
|-------------|--------|---------------------|
| Draft normative source | `https://regulation.gov.ru/projects/166217/` | A Minzdrav project exists to amend the 2026-2028 state-guarantees program for free medical care; the page still surfaces draft-process status rather than a clearly exposed final adopted act |
| Official announcement | `https://max.ru/government_rus/AZ1ie_dfJAY` | `Правительство. Главное` states that OMS-covered oncology care now includes personalized mRNA vaccines, the peptide vaccine `Онкопепт`, and CAR-T therapy |
| Secondary corroboration | `https://www.vedomosti.ru/society/news/2026/04/06/1188231-onkovaktsini-vklyuchili` | Confirms the April 6 timeline and reproduces the same treatment categories |
| Earlier timeline context | `https://www.vedomosti.ru/society/news/2026/03/11/1182199-vaktsinu-ot-raka` | Connects the April announcement to the March 11 draft and records the proposed dosing-schedule language referenced during the draft stage |

## What Is Actually Supported

1. In March 2026, Minzdrav published draft project `166217` on `regulation.gov.ru` for changes to the 2026-2028 state-guarantees program.
2. On April 6, 2026, the official government channel stated that OMS-covered oncology care includes personalized mRNA vaccines, `Онкопепт`, and CAR-T.
3. Secondary reporting corroborates the announcement and its same-day timing.
4. The source stack therefore supports a payer-side policy signal in a major jurisdiction.

## What This Does Not Prove

1. It does not prove nationwide operational rollout is fully verified at site level.
2. It does not prove procurement scale, treatment volume, or reimbursement throughput.
3. It does not prove OpenRNA has market traction or deployment commitments.
4. It does not replace FDA, EMA, ICH, or Part 11 analysis in [REGULATORY_CONTEXT.md](REGULATORY_CONTEXT.md).

## Evidence-Tier Interpretation

For OpenRNA's T1-T4 model, this fits best as **T3 strategic signal**:

- it is stronger than a speculative horizon claim because it is grounded in a primary government announcement plus a traceable draft-policy path;
- it is weaker than T2 validated trajectory because operational implementation and durable reimbursement behavior are not yet directly verified here.

## Why It Matters To OpenRNA

OpenRNA is positioned as a control plane between molecular profiling, neoantigen selection, construct design, review, and manufacturing handoff. A payer-side signal like this matters because it increases the importance of:

- traceability from patient-specific molecular evidence to treatment decisions,
- workflow governance around individualized oncology therapies,
- evidence surfaces that distinguish implemented capability from policy context,
- honest claim boundaries when discussing regulatory or reimbursement relevance.

## Safe Public Claims

- Russia published an April 2026 official announcement linking OMS coverage to personalized mRNA oncology vaccines, `Онкопепт`, and CAR-T.
- There is a draft-policy trail on `regulation.gov.ru` project `166217` behind that timeline.

- Operational rollout and hospital execution are fully verified.
- The announcement proves commercial demand or near-term revenue.
- The source stack by itself upgrades OpenRNA implementation claims from T1/T3 into T2.
